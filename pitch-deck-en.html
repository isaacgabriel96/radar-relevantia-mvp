The trend · where the pain comes from

Attention is fragmenting.
And it's irreversible.

The audience once concentrated in a few channels is now scattered across thousands of niches, formats and creators. From here on, attention will only become more diffuse.

  • Capturing attention is the precondition for any campaign result — and it's getting more expensive, complex and risky to reach.
  • Brands will struggle more and more to deliver results. Not for lack of budget, but because the audience is no longer in one place.
  • It's scattered across Personality, Event, Musical Artist and Media niches.
The more diffuse attention becomes, the harder every investment decision gets. The pain isn't cyclical — it's structural and growing.
Why the status quo won't solve it

Today brands outsource this pain to agencies.
And agencies won't keep up.

✋

Manual & barely data-driven

Most agencies run on relationships, spreadsheets and gut feeling — not data.

⚡

Slower than the market

The market evolves and fragments faster than that model can follow.

🔍

Brands decide blind

They pay a premium, decide without data, and can't measure or control what came back.

It's not a lack of effort from the agency — it's an architecture problem. Diffuse attention demands infrastructure and data, not more human hours.

The unfair advantage

A market won by access.
Not by technology.

This market isn't won with code alone. It's won with access, relationships, reputation and trust — and that can't be bought, only built.

  • Isaac Araujo (founder) spent years building direct relationships with brands and rights holders. That network unlocks supply and demand at the same time.
